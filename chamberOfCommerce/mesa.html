City of Mesa History and Demographics

The city of Mesa and the surrounding region, have been home to varied peoples who have both contributed and left their mark for the residents today. The Hohokam Indians were the first established peoples in the area, and spent at least 1500 years farming and developing the land, using a sophisticated canal system, which they designed and built. These hundreds of miles of canals allowed them to thrive in the desert and were the foundation for the canals which future settlers would use. The Hohokam Indians mysteriously disappeared from the land around 1450 A.D., and the region has no known history until the mid-19th century when the United States Army relocated Apache Indians to the area, and opened the way for settlement.

In 1877 Mormon Pioneers arrived in Mesa, and built several small communities in the Mesa area, one of them being on a mesa overlooking their original fort. The city was incorporated and given its name on July 17, 1878, and by 1883 had 300 residents. The city has gone through many changes, from being primarily an agricultural community to a thriving suburb of Phoenix, with amenities that can make anyone feel right at home.

Today Mesa has a population of over half a million residents, and is the third largest city in Arizona. Mesa prides itself on retaining its small-town feel despite its size, and the downtown area is full of small shops and businesses. From antique stores to clothing shops, to bakeries and cafes, the people and business owners of Mesa will welcome you in. Mesa is home to many museums and archaeological sites, making learning easy and fun. The Cactus League for Major League Spring Training resides in Mesa, and offers an exciting springtime of baseball. The downtown area is home to the Mesa Arts Center, where culture and inclusion combine to offer great performances and learning opportunities. The light rail connects Mesa to Phoenix and offers access to even more sporting and performance events. Mesa has it all, a small-town atmosphere with big city events and attractions. We welcome you!

Interesting Mesa Facts:

  • There is only one high-rise building in Mesa, the Bank of America building
  • The largest Wurlitzer organ in the world is displayed and performed on nightly at Organ Stop Pizza
  • Mesa has sister cities in Canada, Peru, Mexico, China and New Zealand
  • Mesa has over 2280 acres of parkland in the city limits
  • Mesa is located in the Sonoran Desert, home to the Saguaro Cactus
  • Mesa is the largest suburban city by population in the United States
  • The east to west span of Mesa is 18 miles
  • The first school in Mesa was built in 1879
  • Phoenix-Mesa Gateway Airport is the largest relief airport in the Phoenix area
  • Mesa is the largest city in the United States that is not a county seat
